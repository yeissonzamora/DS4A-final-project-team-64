COVID 19 in Bucaramanga city.

Introduction

In the year 2020, health events have occurred worldwide that have led various countries to declare a state of emergency in health matters, due to the high risks of contagion and spread of COVID 19. As this is a virus that attacks the respiratory system, with high spread and contagion, and in some cases even fatal. In this sense, the Ministry of Health and Social Protection through Resolution No. 385 of March 12, 2020, declared a health emergency throughout the national territory until May 30, 2020. In turn, the Governor of the Department of Santander with authorization from the Departmental Risk Management Council declared the Public Calamity in the 87 municipalities of the department, by Decree No. 193 of March 16, 2020. Likewise, the Municipal Mayor of Bucaramanga, by Decree No. 0084 of March 16, 2020, adopted sanitary measures and transitory actions to the preservation of life and risk mitigation, on the occasion of the situation of the declaration of health emergency due to the Coronavirus (COVID 19), and other provisions were issued.

business context.

Business Problem

The mayor's office of Bucaramanga is a public entity that manages and guarantees the correct execution of Bucaramanga city projects in favor of the benefit of the citizens. Due to the COVID19 pandemic, Bucaramanga city was declared under health emergency by the national government. Also, the mayor adopted action and prevention measures. For that reason, the city requires taking advantage of the data provided by health entities in order to characterize the different indicators related to the pandemic to support the decision-making of the city's risk committee. We are interested in discovering and understanding unnoticed trends in COVID19 in the hospitable and epidemiologic fields that may be helpful to improve the current decision making.

Business Impact.

The economic, social, psychological and environmental aspects of Bucaramanga families have been affected by the COVID19 pandemic, this means a direct impact on their economic livelihood. For this reason, the municipal administration has the obligation to guarantee adequate decision-making to reduce the impact on families, providing solutions and strategic actions to minimize the impact and improve living conditions.
